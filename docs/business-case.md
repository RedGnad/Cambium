# Cambium MRV - Business Case

Cambium should be evaluated as privacy-preserving evidence infrastructure, not as a generic farm dashboard and not as a carbon credit issuer.

## 1. Investment Stages

### Stage 0 - Hackathon Prototype

Goal:
Build and demonstrate the full evidence flow.

Estimated cost:

- Cash cost: EUR 0-2,000 if built by founders.
- Time cost: 2-4 weeks focused development.
- Partner contribution: 2-6 hours of field/machine advisory plus one anonymized dataset.

Deliverable:
Working demo, GitHub repo, video, public verification page and PDF report.

### Stage 1 - Field Pilot

Goal:
Run Cambium on 3-10 real or semi-real field sessions.

Estimated cost:

- EUR 5,000-15,000 if mostly founder-built.
- EUR 15,000-40,000 if external dev, security or product help is needed.

Deliverable:
Private pilot with anonymized machine evidence packets, basic audit room and partner feedback.

### Stage 2 - Professional MVP

Goal:
Make Cambium usable by a cooperative, buyer, agritech vendor or auditor.

Estimated cost:

- EUR 50,000-150,000 depending on integration depth.
- Main costs: security, data storage, access control, machine adapter, audit workflows and legal review.

Deliverable:
Production-grade evidence packet system with privacy controls and professional reporting.

### Stage 3 - Commercial V1

Goal:
Sell Cambium as an evidence infrastructure product.

Estimated cost:

- EUR 150,000-400,000+ depending on team size, integrations and certifications.

Deliverable:
Multi-tenant SaaS, machine/gateway adapters, audit room, external corroborations and customer pilots.

## 2. Revenue Models

### Model A - SaaS For Cooperatives / Buyers

Customers:
Cooperatives, food buyers, processors, exporters and RWA issuers.

Pricing hypothesis:

- EUR 500-2,000/month for small pilots.
- EUR 5,000-25,000/year for cooperative or buyer deployments.
- Higher pricing if audit workflows and integrations are included.

Why it works:
The buyer or cooperative has stronger compliance pressure than the individual farmer.

### Model B - Evidence Packet Fee

Customers:
Platforms or operators producing many field events.

Pricing hypothesis:

- EUR 0.50-5 per evidence packet depending on volume and verification depth.
- Higher price for AL3-AL5 dossiers with sensor, calibration or third-party corroboration.

Why it works:
Pricing scales with usage and audit value.

### Model C - Pilot / Integration Fee

Customers:
Agritech vendors, machine operators and cooperatives.

Pricing hypothesis:

- EUR 5,000-30,000 per paid pilot.
- EUR 30,000-100,000+ for custom machine/sensor integration.

Why it works:
Machine-data privacy is not generic SaaS. Integration and trust matter.

### Model D - Audit Evidence Room

Customers:
Auditors, buyers, carbon project developers and RWA due diligence teams.

Pricing hypothesis:

- per dossier;
- per audit room;
- annual compliance workspace.

Why it works:
The value is not the hash. The value is reducing evidence friction during audits.

## 3. ROI Logic

Do not promise guaranteed gain.

Cambium's short-term ROI is not yield improvement. It is evidence readiness.

The value is:

- faster audit preparation;
- stronger buyer trust;
- lower data-sharing risk;
- better green claim substantiation;
- possible premium access to compliant supply chains;
- future readiness for carbon farming or RWA due diligence.

## 4. Commercial Risks

- Buyers may not pay until compliance pressure becomes concrete.
- Farmers may be cautious even with privacy controls.
- Machine vendors may restrict access to useful telemetry.
- Legal framing must stay careful: Cambium supports workflows; it does not certify compliance.
- The first integrations can be services-heavy before they become repeatable software.
- If the packet standard is too complex, pilots will stall.

## 5. Conservative Go-To-Market Wedge

Do not start with "we issue carbon credits".

Start with:

> Privacy-preserving field evidence for audit-heavy agricultural supply chains.

That wedge is narrow enough to demo, specific enough for the field partner and credible enough for Law / Finance / Compliance judges.
